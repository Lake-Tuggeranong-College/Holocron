## Product Demonstration

### Part 1: Product Overview & Presentation (4 minutes)

### Project Overview (1 Minute)

Provide a concise verbal introduction to your project. Cover the following:

- Purpose & Audience: What problem does this solve, and who is it for?
- Current Status: Summarise the project’s current functionality.
- Note: This section is spoken only. No visual aids or slides are to be used for the overview.

### Live Demonstration (3 Minutes)

Conduct a walk-through of the project's key features and technical components.

- Focus: Demonstrate the project’s functionality directly within the development environment.
- Requirements: You must narrate your actions while interacting with the software.
- Note: Slide decks are not to be used during this segment; the focus must remain on the live product.

  

### Part 2: The Development Process (6 minutes)

Transition immediately into a presentation using the provided Google Slides template. Your slides must include diagrams, flowcharts, and evidence from your project to support the following topics:

- Programming Logic: Using a flowchart or pseudocode, describe the logic of the aspect of code [indicated below](https://docs.google.com/document/d/12_8l3sAQuZJn3bjIpU4mwy-k0AOzgqCI9A--BPkmTlg/edit?tab=t.0#heading=h.z5x6v21m9a9m)
- Challenges and Solutions: Highlight any significant challenges you faced and how you addressed them. 

### Programming Logic

Describe the logic of your code (as specified in the table below) using a flowchart or pseudocode. Explain how this logic facilitates the core functionality of your application.

| Subject                         | Code                                              |
| ------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------- |
| Year 11 Robotics & Mechatronics | `main.cpp`                                        |
| Year 11 Networking & Security   | `main.py` within the MCU.                         |
| Year 11 Digital Technologies    | `Player.gd` and interaction with other scripts    |
| Year 12 (All Streams)           | Select one significant script you contributed to. |

### Challenges & Solutions

Identify at least one significant technical or procedural challenge you encountered. Detail the specific steps and troubleshooting methods you used to resolve it.

## Academic Integrity  

All work submitted must be your own; while you may use your peers, family or artificial intelligence to improve your work, the concepts behind your work must be yours. All sources, ideas and quotes should be appropriately referenced. This may include the use of APA and in-text referencing techniques.

Failure to submit original work, alongside appropriate declarations will result in action being taken per BSSS plagiarism policy. If you require assistance with referencing, you should discuss it with your teacher, the teacher​ ​librarians, or visit [www.ltclibrary.com](http://www.ltclibrary.com).
