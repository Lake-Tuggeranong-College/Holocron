Robotic Behaviours refers to the specific actions and responses a robot exhibits in response to its environment and programmed instructions. 

Essentially, it’s how a robot *acts* – it’s more than just moving; it’s about the intelligent execution of tasks. These behaviours are built from a combination of sensor data (like seeing with cameras or feeling with touch sensors), programmed algorithms (the ‘rules’ the robot follows), and sometimes even machine learning, allowing the robot to adapt and improve over time.  
# Examples

| Robotic Behavior              | Description                               | Potential Sensors                               | Potential Actuators                             | Notes                                                              |
|-------------------------------|-------------------------------------------|-------------------------------------------------|-------------------------------------------------|--------------------------------------------------------------------|
| **Precise Component Placement (Robot Arm)** | Placing components accurately on an assembly line. | Force/Torque Sensors (end-effector), Vision Systems (cameras for object recognition & localization), Encoders (joint angles), Proximity Sensors | Robotic Arm Joints (motors), Gripper (pneumatic or electric) | Requires high precision and feedback control.  Often uses visual servoing. |
| **Self-Driving Car Navigation** | Navigating roads, avoiding obstacles, following traffic laws. | LiDAR, Radar, Cameras (stereo vision), GPS, IMU (Inertial Measurement Unit), Ultrasonic Sensors | Steering System, Throttle, Brake System, Acceleration System | Complex behavior requiring sensor fusion and path planning. |
| **Obstacle Avoidance (Cleaning Robot)** | Moving around a room without colliding with objects. | Ultrasonic Sensors, Infrared Sensors, Bump Sensors, LiDAR (lower cost versions), Cameras (for visual obstacle detection) | Motors (for movement), Wheels,  Potential for small brushes/cleaning attachments | Simpler behavior, often relying on reactive sensing. |
| **Voice Command Response (Robotic Dog)** | Responding to commands like "sit" or "fetch." | Microphone (for speech recognition), IMU (for posture detection), Cameras (for visual understanding) | Motors (for leg movement), Actuators for tail movement, Speakers (for vocalization) |  Combines speech recognition with motor control and potentially visual understanding. |
