# Organisational IP, Ethics, & Privacy

---

## 1. The Core Policies

Organisations use policies to protect assets and people.

| Policy | Core Principle | Goal |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **IP** | **Ownership** | Protect original creations (ideas, code, designs). |
| **Ethics** | **Integrity** | Ensure honest, fair, and harmless actions. |
| **Privacy** | **Confidentiality** | Protect personal data from unauthorised access. |

--

## 1.2 Your Role: Observe & Adhere

You are a representative of the organisation.

*   **IP:** Do not copy code or designs for personal use.
    *   *Consequence:* Legal action/Termination.
*   **Privacy:** Do not 'snoop' or leak customer data.
    *   *Consequence:* Fines and loss of trust.
*   **Ethics:** Be honest and report misconduct.
    *   *Consequence:* Reputation damage.

--

## 1.3 The Purpose & Intention

Why do strict rules exist? **Risk Management** and **Trust**.

*   **IP Intention:** Maintain competitive advantage (protect the 'secret sauce').
*   **Privacy Intention:** Build customer trust (safe handling of data).
*   **Ethics Intention:** Ensure fair play and responsibility towards society.

--

## 1.4 Ethical Frameworks

How to make tough choices:

**1. Utilitarianism (Consequences)**
*   *Focus:* The Outcome.
*   *Goal:* The greatest good for the greatest number.

**2. Deontology (Duty)**
*   *Focus:* The Act.
*   *Goal:* Following strict duties and rules, regardless of the outcome.

---

## 2.1 Developing Procedures

Policies are blueprints designed to prevent infringement.

*   **IP Procedures:** IP Assignment Agreements (contracts) to prove ownership.
*   **Ethics Procedures:** Whistleblower hotlines and Codes of Conduct to ensure accountability.
*   **Privacy Procedures:** Access controls (passwords) and data destruction (shredding) to prevent breaches.

--

## 2.2 Key Principles Applied

**IP Principles**
*   *Work-for-Hire:* What you create at work belongs to the employer.
*   *Non-Disclosure:* Keep trade secrets private.

**Ethics Principles**
*   *Fiduciary Duty:* Act in the company's best interest, not yours.
*   *Non-Discrimination:* Treat everyone fairly.

**Privacy Principles**
*   *Data Minimisation:* Collect only what is necessary.
*   *Purpose Limitation:* Don't use data for unapproved reasons.

---

## 3.1 Risk Assessment Process

Organisations proactively manage threats to IP and Ethics.

1.  **Identify:** Locate assets and threats.
2.  **Analyse:** Determine likelihood and impact.
3.  **Evaluate:** Rank risks by severity.
4.  **Treat (Mitigate):** Implement controls to reduce risk.

--

## 3.2 Identifying Non-Compliance

Be observant of infringements:

*   **IP Red Flags:** Competitors using your logo; internal documents on public forums.
*   **Privacy Red Flags:** Staff accessing records of neighbours or friends without cause.
*   **Ethics Red Flags:** Falsifying expense reports or accepting bribes/gifts.

--

## 3.3 Overcoming Incidents

When non-compliance happens, use these principles with the help of relevant personnel:

1.  **Containment:** Stop the damage immediately (e.g., shut down the server).
2.  **Accountability:** Identify who is responsible.
3.  **Transparency:** Inform stakeholders (internal and external).

**Who helps?**
*   *IT/Security:* Technical repairs.
*   *Legal:* Regulatory issues.
*   *HR:* Disciplinary action.

---

## Summary Checklist

*   **Protect:** Ensure work remains company property (IP).
*   **Secure:** Treat personal data with care (Privacy).
*   **Act:** Follow rules and seek the greater good (Ethics).
