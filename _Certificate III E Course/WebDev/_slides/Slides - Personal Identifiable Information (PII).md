# Slide 1: Unit Overview - BSBXCS303

## Securely Manage Personally Identifiable Information and Workplace Information

This unit describes the skills and knowledge required to securely manage Personally Identifiable Information (PII) and confidential workplace data, ensuring compliance with privacy laws and organisational policy.

---

# Element 1: Handle Information Responsibly

## The Foundation of Data Security

This element focuses on identifying and handling sensitive data correctly within the scope of your role.

--

### Performance Criteria

1. **Review Current Standards (1.1):**
    - Review current standards, practices, and procedures related to workplace information management.
2. **Identify Sensitive Data (1.2):**
    - Identify sensitive data in your work environment according to organisational policies and procedures.
3. **Classify Information (1.3):**
    - Classify workplace information types (e.g., Public, Internal, Confidential) according to required organisational procedures.    
4. **Apply Privacy Policies (1.4):**
    - Apply relevant privacy policies to all data devices and documents that require confidentiality.

---

# Element 2: Store and Share Information Securely

## Protecting Data In Motion and At Rest

This element covers the practical steps required to organise, access, and protect data while it is stored or being shared.

--

### Performance Criteria

1. **Organise Data (2.1):**
    - Organise obtained data sets in an easily retrievable format (e.g., standard file paths, naming conventions).
2. **Implement Access Control (2.2):**
    - Implement required access control protocols (e.g., passwords, multi-factor authentication, permission levels) for identified sensitive data.
3. **Ensure Accuracy (2.3):**
    - Confirm that data is accurate, up-to-date, and comprehensive before storage or sharing.
4. **Monitor and Report Threats (2.4):**
    - Identify and report malfunctioning infrastructure or actual/potential attacks that pose a threat to data integrity, following reporting protocols.

---

# Element 3: Apply Information Protection Protocols

## Proactive Compliance and Risk Management

This element requires applying robust protocols to protect information lifecycle-wide, from creation to disposal.

--
### Performance Criteria

1. **Conduct Back-up (3.1):**
    - Conduct back-up of on-site and off-site data according to organisational policies and procedures to ensure business continuity.
2. **Conduct PIAs (3.2):**
    - Conduct Privacy Impact Assessments (PIAs) on data when introducing new systems or processes to proactively manage risks.
3. **Confirm Compliance (3.3):**
    - Confirm adherence to all relevant data protection compliance standards, industry standards, and legislative requirements.


---

# Key Knowledge Evidence Required

## What You Need to Know

To successfully complete this unit, you must demonstrate knowledge of:
- **Legislative Requirements:**
    - Data protection legislation (e.g., Australian Privacy Principles, Notifiable Data Breach scheme).
- **Organisational Policies:**
    - Procedures for identifying, storing, sharing, and managing sensitive information.
    - Protocols for data classification, encryption, and document labelling.
- **Data Protection Protocols:**
    - Industry-standard practices for back-up, data sharing, storage, and secure disposal of sensitive information.
    - The importance of Privacy Impact Assessments (PIAs).
    - How to monitor and report IT infrastructure faults or malfunctions.

---

# Essential Assessment Requirements

## What You Need to Demonstrate

### Performance Evidence

The candidate must demonstrate the ability to:

- Use data protection techniques to manage workplace information for one work area over the life of a project or work cycle.
- Store and share Personally Identifiable Information (PII) in a secure manner.
